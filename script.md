### 📌 Slide 6 — What is AGI? ANI vs AGI

> "OK, so let's break this down simply.
>
> The AI we use today is called **ANI — Artificial Narrow Intelligence**. It's good at ONE thing.
> - Siri can answer questions, but it can't write a scientific paper.
> - Netflix recommends movies, but it can't diagnose a disease.
> - A chess AI beats grandmasters, but ask it to cook you dinner? It has no idea.
>
> **AGI — Artificial General Intelligence** — is different. It's an AI that can do **everything** a human can do — and more. It can learn new things on its own, apply knowledge across different fields, and actually *understand* why things work, not just memorize patterns.
>
> 🗣️ **Think of it like this:** ANI is a tool. AGI is a *mind*."

---

### 📌 Slide 7 — Why the AGI Actually Wins

> "Now, some of you might be thinking: 'OK, but we've had big inventions before. Cars. Internet. Phones. What makes AGI different?'
>
> Great question. Look at this chart.
>
> - The **Agricultural Revolution** took over 10,000 years to fully change civilization.
> - The **Industrial Revolution** took about 250 years.
> - The **Information Revolution** — the internet, computers — about 50 years.
>
> See the pattern? Each revolution happens **faster** than the last. AGI won't take decades. It could unfold in **years**.
>
> That's because AGI doesn't just solve problems — it **improves itself**. And once it starts doing that, the pace of change decouples from human limitations."

### 📌 Slide 9 — The Calculator Era + The Kasparov Moment

> "**Era 1: The Calculator Era (1945–1951)**
>
> The first computers — like ENIAC — were basically giant rooms full of wires. They could do math, but that's it. No learning. No adapting. Zero intelligence.
>
> **Era 2: The Kasparov Moment (1997)**
>
> Then IBM built Deep Blue, and it beat Garry Kasparov — the best chess player in the world. For the first time, a machine outsmarted a human in a game of pure strategy.
>
> 🗣️ **But here's the thing:** Deep Blue didn't *think*. It just calculated millions of moves per second. It was brute force, not intelligence. Still… it was a sign of things to come."

---

### 📌 Slide 10 — AlphaGo + The Transformer

> "**Era 3: AlphaGo (2016)**
>
> The game of Go has more possible board positions than there are atoms in the universe. People said no computer could ever play it well. Then DeepMind's AlphaGo not only played it — it *invented* new strategies that no human had ever seen. It displayed something that looked like **intuition**.
>
> **Era 4: The Transformer (2017)**
>
> Google published a research paper that changed everything. It introduced the **attention mechanism** — a way for AI to understand context, not just process words one by one. This is the foundation of ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — everything.
>
> 🗣️ **In short:** We went from calculators → chess → intuition → language understanding. Each step was a quantum leap."

---

### 📌 Slide 11 — Section 3: The Turning Point & Acceleration

> "Now we enter Section 3 — the turning point. This is where things start to get… fast."

---

### 📌 Slide 12 — The Spark: "Attention Is All You Need"

> "This is the paper that started it all. Published by Google in 2017.
>
> The **attention mechanism** allowed AI to look at an entire sentence at once and understand which words are related to which. Before this, AI read words one at a time, like reading through a keyhole. After this, it could see the whole page.
>
> This is the foundation for GPT-2, GPT-3, GPT-4, and the entire explosion of AI we're living through right now."

---

### 📌 Slide 13 — Trend 1: The Expansion of Attention

> "Here's something wild. Look at how the 'attention span' of AI has grown:
>
> - **GPT-3** could focus for about **15 seconds** of equivalent human effort.
> - **GPT-4** jumped to **4 minutes**.
> - **GPT-5** (projected) can work autonomously for **7 hours straight**.
>
> 🗣️ **What does that mean?** It means AI is going from being a tool you have to babysit… to one that can work on its own. Imagine giving an AI a task in the morning and coming back to a finished project by lunch."

---

### 📌 Slide 14 — Intelligence Trend: IQ Benchmarks

> "Now let's talk about how smart these systems are getting.
>
> Researchers measure AI intelligence using something similar to IQ tests:
> - In 2022, AI scored about **10 IQ** — cognitive impairment level.
> - In 2023, with GPT-4o, it reached about **50 IQ**.
> - In 2025, it's estimated at **70 IQ** — that's a normal human.
> - By 2026, projections show **136 IQ** — that's Albert Einstein level. One of the smartest humans to ever live.
>
> 🗣️ **Let that sink in.** In just 4 years, AI went from cognitively impaired… to Einstein. And it's not slowing down."

---

### 📌 Slide 15 — Trend 2: The Collapse of Cost

> "And here's the other part of the equation: it's getting **cheaper**.
>
> - AI that cost **$1 per million tokens** now costs **$0.01**. That's a 100x reduction.
> - At the 50 IQ level, prices dropped from **$25 to $0.14** in one year.
> - Soon, 70 IQ-level intelligence will cost **fractions of a penny**.
>
> 🗣️ **Translation:** Super-intelligent AI won't just be for big companies. It'll be affordable for *everyone*. Every student. Every worker. Every country."

### 📌 Slide 17 — The Recursive Loop (AlphaEvolve)

>
> 🗣️ **Think about that for a second.** Human engineers are no longer the bottleneck. When AI improves AI, the pace of innovation **decouples from human limitations**. It becomes a loop — AI makes itself smarter, which makes it even better at making itself smarter.
>
> This is the recursive loop. And once it starts, there's no off switch."

## 💡 Quick Tips for Presenting

| Tip | Detail |
|-----|--------|
| 🗣️ **Ask questions** | Every few slides, ask the audience something. Keep them awake. |
| ⏸️ **Pause after big stats** | When you say "136 IQ = Einstein," pause. Let it hit. |
| 🤝 **Make eye contact** | Don't read from the screen. Look at your classmates. |
